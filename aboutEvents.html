ALERT TYPES:

SHOOTING: The Shooting alert can refrence to shooting near or on campus.Regardless, faculty staff and students are to remain indoors until further notice.
This is NOT A DRILL.
BOMB: The Bomb alert can refer to the following: a bomb has been located by someone near or on campus, a bomb has been detonated near or on campus,
a bomb threat has been made near or on campus, REGARDLESS faculty staff and students are to remain indoors until evacuated by law enforcement
TORNADO: The Tornado alert refers that a tornado has been spotted or is about to make landfall near the school premises, evacuate to the basement immediately.
HURRICANE: The Hurricane alert can refer to an Hurricane near the campus or in the campus.
FIRE: The Fire alert is to warn faculty and staff about an uncontrolled fire near or on campus.
EARTHQUAKE: The Earthquake alert can refer to an earthquake in the campus or in the region.
BIOHAZARD: The Biohazard alert can refer to a spill of toxic chemicals near the school. Stay inside until the proper security containment unit has been deployed.
INTRUDER: The Intruder alert is refered to when an individual has been reported to have tresspassed onto the schools premises, stay inside and stay frosty.
FIGHTING: The Fighting alert is used when a physical altercation has broke out near or on campus, call security immediately

Threat-Level:

RED: Critical stay inside, stay safe
ORANGE: Severe, procceed with caution
YELLOW: Moderate, call security
